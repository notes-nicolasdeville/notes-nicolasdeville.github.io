Value Stream Management (VSM)

is a business and software development methodology and practice that aims to optimize the flow of value through an organization's processes, particularly in the context of software development and delivery. It is often associated with Agile and DevOps practices and is used to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of an organization's value delivery processes.

Portfolio Management

strategic planning and oversight of a collection of projects, initiatives, or products within an organization.
It involves making decisions about which projects or products to invest in, how to prioritize them, and how to allocate resources to maximize the overall value delivered to the organization and its customers. Portfolio Management is closely related to VSM because it helps ensure that an organization's investment in various value streams aligns with its strategic goals and objectives.

Agile Planning and Delivery

refers to the set of practices, processes, and methodologies used in Agile software development and project management to plan, prioritize, and deliver work in an iterative and flexible manner. It is a critical component of Agile methodologies such as Scrum, Kanban, and Extreme Programming (XP), which emphasize adaptability, collaboration, and customer-centricity.

Preparation is key

• deep dive on the company, product, and team
• prepare intelligent questions that aren't already answered in the job description

ALWAYS treat it like you want it

Articulate your unique value proposition

• talk in numbers, outcomes, impact. Don't just recite your job responsibilities (value selling vs. feature selling)
• use the power of storytelling
• be brief, to the point, and engaging in your answers

Share stories

Know your numbers

End with a strong close

• be blunt and ask the interviewer for feedback: "what makes me stand out compared to other candidates? what reservations do you have about me so far?"
• address their reservations right away to show off your objection handling skills
• lock in a date for the next step

Follow up

• send an email (or even better: a video) to summarise your main takeaways, how you can add value to the team, and the agreed next step
• add everyone on Linkedin and engage with their content
• follow up if you don't hear anything. Be politely persistent, stay top of mind.
