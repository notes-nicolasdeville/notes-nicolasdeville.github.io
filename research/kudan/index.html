What is SLAM

Commercial-grade Localization and Mapping (SLAM) software is a technology that combines sensor data from various sources such as cameras, lidars, and other sensors to create a map of an environment while simultaneously estimating the robot or vehicle's position within that environment. This technology is widely used in autonomous robots, self-driving vehicles, and drones for navigation and perception tasks.

Commercial-grade SLAM software is designed for use in commercial and industrial applications where high accuracy and robustness are critical requirements. Such software typically includes advanced algorithms and techniques for processing large amounts of data, handling dynamic environments, and maintaining accuracy even in the presence of noise and uncertainties.

Examples of commercial-grade SLAM software include ROS Navigation Stack, GMapping, RTAB-Map, and Cartographer. These software packages are widely used by robotics and autonomous vehicle companies for their mapping and localization needs.
