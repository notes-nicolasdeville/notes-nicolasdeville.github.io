CRO role assessment.

Company

Founded in Poland in 2017 (HQ in Warsaw) with the mission to build an MLOps metadata store for machine learning practitioners that run a lot of experiments.

Neptune.ai offers an MLOps solution that makes it easy to store, organize, display, and compare all metadata generated during model development Unlike notes, organization protocols, or open source tools, it is lightweight, automatic and is a single place maintained by Neptune ai allowing people to focus on actually doing ML rather than metadata bookkeeping.

Following their successful start being listed as a Top 100 AI start up in 2021 2022 Neptune ai recently raised 8 M in a Series A funding round led by Almaz Capital, btov Partners, Rheingau Founders, and TDJ Pitango now Neptune ai is ready to define and own the “ML metadata store” category of the market serving a fast growing customer base.

Mission

Neptune.ai wants to give production ML teams the same level of control and confidence when building models as software developers have when shipping applications

Vision

Neptune is the market standard metadata store for MLOps.

While most companies in the MLOps space try to go wider and become platforms that solve all the problems of machine learning teams, we want to go deeper and become the best-in-class tool for experiment tracking and model registry.

What is Neptune.ai?

Neptune is a platform for tracking, organizing, and sharing machine learning (ML) model metadata. It allows users to log, compare, and register ML model metadata in one place, making it easier for teams to collaborate and standardize their work. Users can deploy Neptune on-premises, in a private cloud, or use the hosted version. It is compatible with any MLOps stack and offers various integrations. With Neptune, users can log ML metadata, organize experiments, compare results, register models, and share their findings with others. Neptune is used by thousands of ML engineers and data scientists, and integrates with popular ML frameworks like PyTorch, Keras, and scikit-learn.

Who is it for?

Neptune is designed for individuals and teams working on machine learning and data science projects, including:

  • Machine Learning Engineers: who develop, train, and optimize ML models, and need a way to track and manage their experiments and model metadata.
  • Data Scientists: who analyze data, create models, and need a platform to organize, compare, and share their work with colleagues or clients.
  • Research Teams: who conduct experiments with various models and configurations, and require a centralized system to log, organize, and analyze their research findings.
  • ML Team Leads or Managers: who oversee machine learning projects and need a way to monitor progress, manage resources, and collaborate with their teams.
  • Organizations: that implement machine learning projects across different departments and require a platform for collaboration, standardization, and sharing of ML model metadata.

Examples of ML metadata

Machine learning model metadata refers to any information related to a model's training, evaluation, and deployment. Some examples of ML model metadata that can be logged using Neptune are:

Hyperparameters

The parameters used to configure a model during training, such as learning rate, batch size, number of layers, and activation functions.

Training Metrics

Metrics measured during the training process, such as loss, accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score.

Validation Metrics

Metrics measured during the validation process to evaluate the model's performance on unseen data, like validation loss and validation accuracy.

Model Artifacts

Files related to the model, such as the trained model weights, architecture, and any pre-processing or post-processing scripts.

Data Versions

Information about the dataset used for training and validation, such as dataset size, splits, and version numbers.

Training Logs

Logs related to the training process, such as epoch numbers, learning curves, and any relevant error messages or warnings.

Visualizations

Charts, graphs, and plots generated during the model development process, like confusion matrices, ROC curves, and feature importance plots.

Environment Information

Details about the software and hardware used during model development, including library versions, operating systems, and hardware configurations.

By logging all these metadata, users can better understand, analyze, and reproduce their ML models, making it easier to collaborate, share, and optimize their work.
