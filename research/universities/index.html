Different types of universities

Category Description
Research Universities Universities that focus on research as well as teaching. They have extensive research facilities and a large number of research faculty members.
Liberal Arts Colleges Colleges that focus on providing a well-rounded education in various subjects such as humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences.
Business Schools Schools that specialize in offering business and management education.
Law Schools Schools that offer legal education and training for students who want to become lawyers.
Medical Schools Schools that offer medical education and training for students who want to become physicians.
Engineering Schools Schools that specialize in offering engineering education and training for students who want to become engineers.
Arts and Design Schools Schools that focus on providing education and training in various fields of art and design.
Community Colleges Colleges that offer two-year programs in various subjects such as business, nursing, and technology.
Vocational Schools Schools that specialize in providing vocational education and training in various fields such as automotive technology, culinary arts, and cosmetology.
Online Universities Universities that offer online programs and courses in various fields of study.

Administration

Each university has a governing body, which is responsible for overseeing the strategic direction and management of the institution.

The governing body is typically made up of:

  • senior academics
  • external experts
  • representatives from industry and the community.

The day-to-day management of the university is carried out by a team of senior executives, including the Vice-Chancellor (or President), equivalent to the CEO of the institution.

Events organised by universities

Universities organize a wide range of events, from academic conferences and research seminars to social and cultural activities for students and the wider community.

Example:

Academic conferences and symposiums

Universities often host conferences and symposiums that bring together academics and researchers from different disciplines and institutions to share their research and discuss new developments in their fields.

Open days and campus tours

Universities hold open days and campus tours to give prospective students and their families the opportunity to explore the campus, meet staff and students, and learn more about the academic programs and support services on offer.

Student fairs and career events

Universities may organize student fairs and career events to help students explore different career paths, meet with potential employers, and learn about job opportunities.

Cultural and arts events

Many universities have their own cultural and arts programs that organize events such as art exhibitions, music concerts, theater productions, and film screenings.

Public lectures and talks

Many universities organize public lectures and talks by leading academics, experts, and public figures on a range of topics, from science and technology to social and political issues.

Community outreach events

Universities may organize events to engage with the local community, such as volunteering initiatives, charity fundraisers, and educational outreach programs for local schools.

These are just some examples of the types of events that universities may organize. The specific events and activities offered by each institution will vary depending on factors such as its size, location, and focus of study.

Separat business unit for Events

Some universities have their own “Events” business unit (or separate company?) to leverage the campus’ premises & resources to organise events for others.

eg.:

  • WithUs, part of The University of Sheffield https://withus.com/conferences-meetings/
  • Imperial Venues, part of Imperial College https://www.imperialvenues.co.uk/
  • BlueBox Events, part of TU Delft https://blueboxevents.nl/
