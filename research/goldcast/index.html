23 Jan 2023

Notes about the Goldcast platform from attending a couple sessions of the MegaSKO event (https://events.modernsaleshq.com/the-mega-sko).

Positives

✅ agenda automatically in user's timezone
✅ Love that the link I had in my invitation led me straight to the webinar playing. No extra step/click.
✅ And the link doesn't have plain text identification of user eg https://events.goldcast.io/splash/6168de3b-edfa-4525-bff6-c01849bc25b5
✅ like the Sponsors page appearing automatically at the end of the webinar. Good way to maxisimise traffic to sponsors' pages.
✅ being in the following session automatically at the end of the webinar.
✅ being brought automatically to a Live event starting while being in a different section
✅ audio intro on the Sponsors page is good to have - but should not loop more than once
✅ GIFs in chat
✅ emojis using macOS native set (instead of limited ugly ones like with most others)
✅ integration of 3rd party tools (eg Photobooth). More available?
✅ Simulive (Jason Lemkin's session was already Live a few weeks ago)

Good move on putting "Powered by Goldcast" at the top of the webinar page. Higher visibility & brand awareness than usual place at the bottom.
Also good to add the "rate Goldcast" NPS style widget, though it's hard to see/find and could benefit from the ability to submit suggestions.

Negatives

❌ webinar interface could be customisable, eg select what to view in the main view (eg speaker instead of slides)
❌ ability to add all sessions or bulk select to add to calendar
❌ Agenda: speaker names / Company / Title should be much more prominent.
❌ on agenda page, opening speaker's profile could happen with 1 click only
❌ Chat UI (at least as setup here) is hard to read
❌ sponsor pages ("booths") are extremely basic: UI overall, lack of branding, resources not well displayed/promoted, etc... (feels like a quick & dirty repurpose of the webinar interface to call it a "booth")
❌ same with Speaker pages/pop up, too basic ❌ recordings on Youtube? No recorded session on the platform?
❌ moderator ending the call should switch entire user console to holding slide instead of havind each speakers hang on and close their own video feed like a meeting.

Question of taste: presenters' video feeds' windows are too rounded.

Improvement ideas

  • play the webinar from start immediately at the end: captures audience members who joined late for more viewing/engagement.
  • easier way to access all recorded sessions
  • Chat UI: assign different colors and/or random avatars to each member for readibility. (also, pre-assigning avatars might prompt more members to set their own)
  • Chat UI: enable direct contact/connect between attendees.
  • Chat UI: threaded chat.
  • more branding/customization options for the organiser (and their sponsors)
  • seeing how many / who is in the webinar too
  • resizing the PIP when navigating agenda, Speakers, etc.. during a Live session could be resizeable
  • Linkedin connects could be streamlined between members (eg 1 click to visit member's page) to avoid that hacky way:
  • better highlight of who is speaking in the webinar interface
  • analytics: tracks when user audio is muted? (I left the window open for hours myself, but unmuted only for some sections - skews the engagement metrics)
  • live transcript

All these notes are based mostly on comparison with other existing traditional solutions. A rethinking of the UX, departing from the norm, could help differentiate a new solution even further - eg dynamic view of other participants in front as if sitting in an auditorium,
