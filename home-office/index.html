Using a teleprompter for business users

improves online presence & experience, for both sides

19 Jul 2022 | in home office

Using a teleprompter has been part of my home office stack since 2020, at the onset of the pandemic.

With everything and everyone moving to online meetings, I wanted to step up my game with regard to my experience with them.

This included revamping my home office entirely (see Nic Note: My Home Office Setup), adding a video mixer (see Nic Note: ATEM Mini for business users), a programmable keypad (Nic Note: Streamdeck for business users) and a teleprompter.

At that time, I was also learning video editing (see Nic Note: Producing videos with Screenflow), as video communications are becoming more important than ever to master. And I always wanted to have the skills to be able to produce quickly videos that can help me in my (Sales) work - product demos, video answers to RFPs,

Pain I was solving:
- looking people "in the eye" during online meetings - making it easy to record more effective (Sales) videos without straying, looking ackwardly at notes, etc..

An app is needed to mirror your text, as a teleprompter works by reflecting (ie inverting) the image from the screen you use (be an iPad or a monitor like me).
The one I use is Promptsmart - see Nic Note: Promptsmart - the teleprompter companion.
