Looking into replacing non-stick Teflon frying pans (from Tefal) as they are not good for your health. Teflon it appears is a carcinogen.

Thinking about investing in cast iron cookware. Le Creuset - Made in France! - is the obvious choice for me.

Found out about enambel coated pans though, instead of pure cast iron.

Is enamel cookware safe to use in your eco-kitchen? | beeco
beeco.green
https://www.beeco.green/facts/enamel-cookware/

Cast iron

  • heating properties: retains heat well.
  • cooking properties: distributes heat evenly.
  • cleaning properties: easy to clean.
  • durability: lasts a lifetime.
  • health properties: no toxic chemicals.
because it is made of unprotected metal, it releases a certain amount of iron into the food being cooked on it.
For some people this may be beneficial, particularly if they are anaemic. However, for others the excess iron can be harmful.
