The word "synthesizer" comes from the Greek word "synthesis," which means "to put together" or "to combine." In the context of electronic music, a synthesizer is a musical instrument that combines different sound waves to create a new sound.

The first electronic synthesizer was invented in the early 20th century, but the term "synthesizer" didn't come into common use until the 1960s and 1970s, when electronic music became more popular.

Top brands

Moog

Founded in the 1960s, Moog is one of the most iconic brands in the history of synthesizers. Their analog synthesizers are renowned for their warm, fat sound and are still used by musicians today.

Korg

Korg is a Japanese company that produces a wide range of synthesizers, from affordable entry-level models to high-end professional instruments. They are known for their innovative features and intuitive interfaces.

Roland

Another Japanese company, Roland has been producing synthesizers since the 1970s. They are known for their versatility and range of sounds, and their instruments have been used in a variety of musical genres.

Yamaha

Yamaha produces a wide range of musical instruments, including synthesizers. Their instruments are known for their high-quality sound and build, and they offer a range of models for both beginners and professionals.

Sequential

Founded by synth pioneer Dave Smith, Sequential is known for their analog synthesizers that combine vintage sound with modern features.

Arturia

Arturia is a French company that produces software and hardware synthesizers. Their instruments are popular with both beginners and professionals and are known for their affordable prices and authentic sound.
