How to access the darknet

A simple guide, for curiosity and research purposes only

05 Sep 2022 | in helpers

04 Jul 2022

Plenty of resources online to access the darknet. Just a quick and practical guide from experience.

FOR CURIOSITY AND RESEARCH PURPOSES ONLY.

Accessing the darknet means using a different browser (TOR), with different URLs (links) using the .onion suffix, and taking precautions to remain anonymous.

How anonymous (and safe) you will be, depends on your computer and connection setup.

Computer

From least to most secure:

  1. using your computer as usual (not recommended)
  2. setup a new blank user with minimal rights and use this user's session (better)
  3. use a different "virgin" computer with Windows/Mac. For example an old one you have reset, in an anonymous way, eg with profile nickname, not connected any service or iCloud, etc.. (even better)
  4. use the TAILS operating system (https://tails.boum.org/), which can run on a USB stick, from any computer. (best approach)

With TAILS, you simply "Shut down the computer and start on your Tails USB stick instead of starting on Windows, macOS, or Linux. Tails leaves no trace on the computer when shut down."

Browser: TOR

TOR = "The Onion Router"
is a version of the Firefox browser, modified to access the Onion network
using .onion links (instead of .com, .net, etc..).

The analogy with an onion stems from its layers - because connecing via TOR means your connection will "hop" from one node to another randomly to reach its destination.
It's like in movies when the protagonist is trying to lose someone tailing them, by changing cars multiple times.

If you're using TAILS it should be pre-installed. Else you can download it here: https://www.torproject.org/

If you want to be extra careful and ensure the one you are downloading is the original, and not someone in the middle serving you an ill-intended copy (unlikely, but theoratically possible), you can verify the signature: https://support.torproject.org/tbb/how-to-verify-signature/.

Connection

From least to most secure:

  1. using your internet connection as usual
